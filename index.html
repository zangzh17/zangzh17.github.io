Research Interests

My research spans optical systems design, lens design and computational imaging with a focus on next-generation sensing technologies. I specialize in the design and fabrication of diffractive optical elements (DOEs) and freeform optics, developing end-to-end optimization methods that integrate lithography physics with gradient-based design. My current work at the iOptics Lab includes ultrafast and hyperspectral optical imaging for biomedical applications, light-field microscopy for biomedical imaging, advanced LiDAR systems with novel beam steering mechanisms, and physics-based modeling using differentiable ray-tracing. I'm particularly interested in bridging the gap between optical hardware and computational algorithms to enable new imaging capabilities in biophotonics and biomedical optics.

Highlight Research Projects

Light-Field Microscopy for Biomedical Imaging

Developing advanced optical architecture for light-field microscopy achieving 5× resolution enhancement via compressive sensing. Designed custom NIR-II objective lens (1000–1300 nm, NA = 0.5) with GPU-accelerated reconstruction pipeline for >600 fps volumetric imaging at the iOptics Lab, focusing on ultrafast biomedical imaging applications.

Fabrication-Integrated DOE Design

Developed novel end-to-end optimization framework linking lithography physics to gradient-based optical design. Enables direct optimization of fabrication processes for 256-level diffractive optical elements with unprecedented accuracy.

Next-Generation LiDAR Systems

Pioneering spectral-scanning FMCW LiDAR with diffractive FOV expansion and solid-state 2D beam steering. Demonstrated parallel single-pixel LiDAR reaching 100k fps depth capture using spectro-temporal encoding.
